Le registre

Entre 1980 et 2000, la photographie de mode a traversé une révolution complète. De l'opulence saturée des années Reagan à la vérité rugueuse du grunge britannique. De la domination des supermodels à l'ère de la perfection numérique.

30 prompts. 5 territoires. Helmut Newton et Corinne Day ne faisaient pas le même travail — mais GPT Image 2 comprend les deux avec la même précision, à condition qu'on lui donne le bon vocabulaire. Ces prompts sont ce vocabulaire.

Chaque prompt nomme son photographe référent, son film stock, sa décennie, sa lumière — pour que GPT Image 2 produise non pas une image générique "mode années 80" mais la grammaire précise de Helmut Newton en 1983 ou de Corinne Day en 1993.

Aperçu des prompts

I — Studio

#001 — Studio Années 80

La lumière de Newton

A high fashion studio portrait, early 1980s aesthetic. The visual grammar of Helmut Newton at his most controlled: a woman in a powerful, structured outfit — broad shoulders, strong silhouette — lit by a single large softbox from the left, a silver reflector balancing the right. The background is seamless white or pale gray. The pose is authoritative, not inviting — the model looks directly into the camera with complete self-possession. 4x5 large format aesthetic, no grain, extraordinary detail.

Newton a imposé la femme puissante comme sujet — pas objet — de la photographie de mode.

#004 — Studio Années 90 — Grunge

La beauté sans permission

A 1990s studio fashion shot in the grunge or heroin chic aesthetic — the deliberately unglamorous beauty that Corinne Day and Davide Sorrenti brought to fashion photography. A model photographed on cheap seamless or a bare wall, available tungsten light, no professional lighting setup. The clothes are deliberately undone. The model's expression is vacant, or somewhere private. The image looks like it was taken in an apartment rather than a studio. 35mm film, slight underexposure.

Le "heroin chic" a choqué parce qu'il montrait la mode sans son mensonge.

II — Extérieur

#009 — Extérieur Quotidien Années 90

La mode dans la vraie vie

A 1990s outdoor fashion shoot that deliberately places high fashion clothing in ordinary, non-glamorous environments — supermarkets, bus stops, parking lots, public housing. The Corinne Day and early Kate Moss aesthetic: fashion photographed where real people actually are. Available light, 35mm, slight grain. The environment is not beautified — it is simply used as found. British independent fashion photography of the early 1990s.

Corinne Day a sorti la mode du studio et des palaces — et l'a mise dans la rue.

III — Backstage · IV — Défilé · V — Éditorial

#014 — Portrait Backstage · #022 — Runway Années 90 · #026 — Éditorial Conceptuel — Accès complet

A backstage portrait of a model between looks — partially made up, one outfit half-removed. The face is in a private state: not performing for the camera, not performing for the audience. Available backstage light, 35mm, slight underexposure. This is the face that fashion usually hides: the professional at rest, the person behind the persona. Analog imperfection is intentional.
